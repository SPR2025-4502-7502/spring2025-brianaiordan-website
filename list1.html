7 best young adult dystopian books that everyone should read

Dystopia is not dead. The classics live on. Here are seven must-read young adult dystopian books that will take you on adventures to vast and vivid new worlds.

The Hunger Games

By Suzanne Collins

The Hunger Games is set in the dystopian, post-apocalyptic nation of Panem. The story follows 16-year-old Katniss Everdeen, who volunteers in place of her sister, Primrose, to compete in the 74th annual Hunger Games. Each of the twelve districts are forced by the Capitol to send one girl and one boy, aged 12 to 18, to fight to the death in a deadly arena. And there can only be one victor.

Legend

By Marie Lu

Legend tells the story of June, a brilliant prodigy, and Day, an elusive criminal, who meet in a twist of fate. June’s brother, Metias, is murdered and all signs point toward Day. She hunts for Day in the slums of the futuristic, militarized nation called the Republic. The two form an unlikely alliance as they uncover the sinister secrets lurking behind the surface — and just how far the Republic is willing to go to conceal the truth.

Click here to order Legend now

Red Queen

By Victoria Aveyard

In a world divided by blood, Mare Barrow, who has Red blood, never expected to be special like the Silver-blooded elite. While the Silvers possess supernatural abilities, Mare is a Red thief from a poor village. In an accidental mishap, Mare discovers she has a power of her own. To cover up this incident, she is forced into the world of royal Silvers, pretending to be one of them. Behind the scenes, a revolution is brewing, and Mare is forced to choose between her head and her heart.

Click here to order Red Queen now

Divergent

By Veronica Roth

Divergent takes place in a dystopian society divided by five factions based on virtues: Abnegation, Amity, Candor, Dauntless, and Erudite. When 16-year-old Beatrice Prior finds out she is “Divergent” and possesses many different virtues, she has a decision to make. Despite her Abnegation roots, she chooses Dauntless. However, she soon discovers that in a society that values conformity, her Divergence doesn’t make her special — it makes her a target.

Click here to order Divergent now

The Maze Runner

By James Dashner

The Maze Runner follows Thomas, who wakes up in an expansive, menacing maze filled with dangerous Grievers. Thomas has no memory of his life before the maze. In the Glade, he is surrounded by a group of boys who have been trying to escape for years. Thomas quickly learns that there’s only one way out, and that’s through the maze. Except no one has ever made it out alive.

Click here to order The Maze Runner now

The Testing

By Joelle Charbonneau

The Testing showcases a dystopian future in which only the brightest students can be selected to attend the United Commonwealth’s prestigious university. Cia Vale has wanted nothing more than to follow in her father’s footsteps and participate in The Testing. But when her dreams become a reality after graduation day, Cia discovers that The Testing is far deadlier than the grueling pencil and paper exams she had expected — and one wrong answer could be her last.

Click here to order The Testing now

The 5th Wave

By Rick Yancey

The 5th Wave takes place in a world devastated by alien invasions, which occur in a series of waves. So far, the waves have left humanity without power, ravaged by natural disasters, and plagued with disease. Survivor Cassie Sullivan is on the run, searching for her brother, Sammy, who has been kidnapped. Cassie must fight to find her brother before it is too late. The fifth wave is approaching — and survival isn’t guaranteed.
